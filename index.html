Non-Chemical-Based Crop Control (NCBCC)

A Market-Driven Approach to Farm Sustainability

Prepared for: Congressional Sub-Committee on Agriculture | November 2025

Proposal: Fund U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA) Grants to Promote Healthy Farms

USDA grants will expand research, outreach, and farmer training on alternative methods.

GOAL: Shift financial incentives from chemical reliance to sustainable, cost-saving, and health-protective agricultural practices.

The Problem: High Cost of Chemical Reliance

Annual Public Health Costs

$42.0 Billion1

Public Health & Equity Crisis:

Pesticides cause increased rates of health conditions impacting vulnerable populations who work and live near pesticides.

Ineffective Regulation:

Policy and markets portray chemical usage as safe and cost-effective, rendering regulation ineffective.

Current State: Industrial Chemical Reliance

The Solution: Boost Farmer Adoption of NCBCC

Budget Neutral

Redirect subsidies

Targeted Investment

Fund R&D & Training

NCBCC Adoption

Sustainable Farms

Innovative Alternatives in Action:

Example: Biological Control Agents
  • Pest Mating Disruption
  • Cover Crop Strategies
  • Predatory Organism Release

Programs like CAFF's BIOS have proven cost-effectiveness2.

The Benefits: Financial & Societal Returns

Farmer Benefits (Financial)

  • Long-Term Savings: Reduces reliance on costly chemical inputs.
  • Market Opportunity: Supports a growing market for safe, viable crop control.

Community Benefits

  • Improves Public Health: Protects vulnerable communities and children.
  • Food Supply: Reduces consumer exposure to pesticide residues.

"Promoting NCBCC measures through existing USDA grant-making frameworks is the most effective way to overcome regulatory shortcomings and usher in a new era of responsible agriculture."
